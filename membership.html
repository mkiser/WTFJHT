Editorial Policy: This is the news through the lens of a person making decisions about coverage – much the same way a front page editor does for newspapers. With every blurb I write, I'm make a news-value judgement. There's a natural tension here: First, humans making decisions about the relative importance of information is inherently biased. And second, journalism is a set of ethics based on trust, truthfulness, fairness, integrity, independence, and accountability. My goal with WTF Just Happened Today? is to acknowledge my personal bias, reflect journalistic standards, and apply the following standards and practices:

  1. WTFJHT takes a neutral-ish position. It's not intentionally partisan. It's purposefully fact-based. It focuses on clearly and concisely saying what happened, citing the primary sources, and moving on. Bias is the byproduct of being a human making decisions.
  2. WTFJHT doesn't offer commentary, opinion, or analysis. Adjectives and adverbs are generally stripped out of the writing so facts are framed as fact. It's unncessary to add a "just" in front of 8% of Americans approve of the job the president is doing. Any reasonable person can understand that that's probably not a good job approval rating, right?
  3. WTFJHT's value is derived from its reputation and promise to the readers. The voice, tone, consistency, and being a reasonable person are all in service of building trust. If I break this promise, I break the trust of the reader.

Above all else, with WTFJHT you can expect an independent voice that is personally accountable and considerate for how the editorial choices impact others. I go to great lengths to cite the original source of news and draw from a diversity of sources.
